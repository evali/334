Process

We'd love to show you how we designed Ignition

Year: 2015

Domain: Car Maintenance

Project type: Interface design for a mobile app

Project Brief:

Research, propose, design, prototype, evaluate and refine an interactive system designed for a specific domain and context.

Why did we choose this domain?

many opportunities and pain points in the field of car maintenance

no dominant technology for users new to the scene

a unique domain that the team was interested in learning and tackling

Who is this for?

Car owners who are curious or want to be experts in maintenance of their cars

What are we making
to solve this problem?

A mobile app that allows users to identify car issues, find tools, and fix it themselves.

Why this approach?

We are reducing the overwhelming process car diagnostics by making it more efficient and as well as help users to get motivated to fix small issues themselves, saving them money and gaining experience.

How are we approaching
this problem?

providing meaningful information to allow them to make informed decisions

identifying problems through OBDKey code or by inputting the symptom

suggesting tools and parts and where to get them

showing easy to understand instructions with videos to accompany every step

expressing encouragement through tone and copy that this issue can be done easily

Research

What exists?

  • OBD key readers for diagnosing the car
  • Software and websites that help you identify the issues

What doesn't exist?

  • Software and websites that focus on DIY and use step-by-step instructions
  • A continuous workflow, avoiding the need to switch between interfaces
  • Information about severity and whether users are able to fix it themselves
  • Provide encouragement and motivation to users
  • Evaluation of steps

We conducted user interviews and online surveys on car owners to gather insights about the workflow of car maintenance and their motivations.

We identified a three stage user journey: before, during, and after car maintenance

User Testing

Process: usability testing and interview

  • Think aloud, in-context, post-test questionnaire
  • usability testing
  • navigation confusion between main navigation bar, progress bar, and hamburger menu
  • interaction in Find Tools workflow was confusing as there was a conflict between toolbox interaction and shopping interaction
  • validated that experts like to skip steps and view all steps at once
  • beginners like to do it step by step to avoid overwhelming
  • app motivated them

Refinements

iterations after user testing and crit feedback emphasize what tools are being used for each step fixed navigation bar issue removed hamburger menu and consolidated the toolbox and history into the home dashboard to remove the navigation issue and provide meaningful information at a glance

Future Opportunities

expand to other vehicles (boats, motorbikes)

explore different types of interaction (motion, voice)
